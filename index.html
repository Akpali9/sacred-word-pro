        The Apocrypha — Historical Context

        The Apocrypha (Greek: apókryphos — "hidden things") are ancient texts written between roughly 300 BCE and 100 CE. They were part of the Septuagint — the Greek Old Testament used by early Christians — and quoted by Church Fathers for centuries.

        All King James Bibles published before 1666 included the Apocrypha. The Catholic Church declared them canonical at the Council of Trent (1546). Protestant reformers gradually removed them, though Luther himself called them "useful and good to read."

        They remain fully canonical in Catholic, Eastern Orthodox, and Ethiopian Orthodox traditions to this day.
